# Customer Churn Prediction & Business Analytics using SQL, Python & Machine Learning

##  Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing customer churn and building machine learning models to predict customers who are likely to leave a telecom company. The project combines SQL, Python, data analysis, visualization, and machine learning to uncover key churn drivers and provide actionable business recommendations.

The workflow covers the complete data analytics lifecycle—from SQL analysis and data cleaning to predictive modeling and business insights.

---

#  Objectives

- Analyze customer behavior and churn patterns
- Clean and preprocess raw customer data
- Perform exploratory data analysis (EDA)
- Build multiple machine learning classification models
- Compare model performance
- Identify the key factors driving customer churn
- Provide business recommendations to improve customer retention

#  Executive Summary

### Key Business Insights

- Customers on month-to-month contracts showed the highest churn rate.
- Customers with shorter tenure were more likely to leave.
- Higher monthly charges were associated with increased churn.
- Lack of additional services such as technical support and online security contributed to higher churn.
- Contract type and tenure were among the strongest predictors of customer churn.

---

#  Business Recommendations

- Offer loyalty rewards for long-term customers.
- Encourage customers to switch to longer-term contracts.
- Provide targeted retention campaigns for high-risk customers.
- Bundle value-added services such as technical support.
- Monitor high monthly charge customers and offer personalized plans.

---

#  Machine Learning Outcome

The trained models successfully predicted customer churn, enabling the identification of customers at high risk of leaving. Feature importance analysis helped explain the key factors influencing churn and provided actionable insights for customer retention strategies.
